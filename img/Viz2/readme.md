# Visualization 2 — Global Fish Production (Capture vs Aquaculture)
This visualization compares trends in capture fisheries and aquaculture production, showing that capture fisheries have remained relatively stable while aquaculture has grown rapidly. This indicates that global fish supply is increasingly being driven by aquaculture rather than wild fish stocks, which appear to have reached their ecological limits. The divergence between the two trends highlights the growing reliance on farmed fish to meet rising global demand.

This matters for the decision because it reinforces the idea that continued dependence on wild fisheries is not sustainable in the long term. As demand for seafood increases, aquaculture will play a critical role in maintaining supply without further depleting natural ecosystems. For decision-makers, this underscores the need to support sustainable aquaculture practices while implementing stricter management of capture fisheries to prevent overexploitation.
