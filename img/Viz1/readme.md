# Visualization 1 — Aquaculture Employment (Fish Farmers)
This visualization shows the growth in global aquaculture employment over time, highlighting how the number of fish farmers has steadily increased. The upward trend indicates that aquaculture is becoming a major source of livelihoods, particularly as pressure on wild fish stocks limits expansion in capture fisheries. This reveals a structural shift in the global fishing industry, where production and employment are increasingly tied to controlled farming systems rather than natural ecosystems.

This matters for the decision because it suggests that reducing fishing pressure on wild stocks does not necessarily mean reducing economic opportunities. Instead, aquaculture can serve as an alternative pathway to support employment while promoting sustainability. For decision-makers, this highlights the importance of investing in aquaculture development and training programs to offset potential job losses from stricter fishing regulations, helping balance economic and environmental priorities.
